# Otology Agent Extra Smoke Test

Twelve additional full-pipeline questions for validating hybrid retrieval outside the canonical benchmark set.

## Questions

### 1. Hybrid Retrieval Smoke

1. In children with recurrent acute otitis media but no middle ear effusion at evaluation, what is the evidence for tympanostomy tubes versus watchful waiting?
2. For idiopathic sudden sensorineural hearing loss presenting after two weeks, what evidence supports salvage intratympanic steroid therapy?
3. In chronic suppurative otitis media, how do topical quinolone drops compare with systemic antibiotics for resolving otorrhea and improving hearing?
4. What is the evidence for autoinflation devices in children with otitis media with effusion?
5. For acquired cholesteatoma, how do canal wall up and canal wall down mastoidectomy compare for residual or recurrent disease and hearing outcomes?
6. What evidence compares endoscopic ear surgery with microscopic tympanoplasty for tympanic membrane closure and hearing outcomes?
7. What factors predict hearing outcomes after ossiculoplasty with partial or total ossicular replacement prostheses?
8. For small vestibular schwannomas, what evidence compares observation, stereotactic radiosurgery, and microsurgery for hearing preservation?
9. In adults with obstructive Eustachian tube dysfunction, what is the durability of benefit after balloon dilation and which patients were studied?
10. What is the evidence for ototoxicity monitoring protocols in patients receiving aminoglycosides or cisplatin?
11. In acute otitis media, can a 20-month-old with nonsevere unilateral disease be managed with watchful waiting, and what follow-up is required?
12. Why do children under 2 years of age require prompt treatment rather than watchful waiting for acute otitis media?
